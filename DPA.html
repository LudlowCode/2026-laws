Data Protection Act 2018

The DPA follows on from two other notable pieces of legislation, the DPA 1998 and the EU's GDPR. Their aim is to protect the data subjects from harm caused by inappropriate storage, use or harvesting of their data.

Roles

  • Data subject is the person who is having their data stored
  • Data controller is the person in the company/organisation who is in charge of data use
  • Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) is the public body that oversees data usage

The 8 principles of Data Protection

  1. Specify reasons
  2. Collect and use fairly
  3. Use for intended purposes
  4. Ensure accuracy
  5. Keep secure
  6. Ensure adequate but not excessive scope
  7. Minimise duration of storage
  8. Keep data within DPA/GDPR jurisdiction

Data subject

As the data subject, you have legal rights thanks to GDPR. You have the right to:

  • Access your data
  • Have your data corrected
  • Protection from distress
  • Protection from direct marketing
  • Protection from automated decision-making
  • Complain to the ICO
  • Compensation

Information Commissioner's Office

This is the public body responsible for making sure that companies follow the DPA.
